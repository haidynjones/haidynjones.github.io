Country Swing Dancing

What is Country Swing Dancing?

According to the Library of Dance,

Library of Dance

"Country Swing is a popular American partner dance which combines rapid-fire Swing moves with awe-inspiring dips and lifts and the anything goes attitude of Blues."

The Library of Dance is a collection of social dance variations and music.

Swing dancing is usually done on crowded dance floors in western themed bars. Here in Idaho, there are three main country bars people can go to learn how to country swing or just social dance.

  • ICS
  • The Buffalo Club
  • Cowgirls

Indian Creek Steakhouse is located in Caldwell, ID just off of the freeway in the Indian Creek Plaza. This is a great place for those who are under the age of 21 to go learn how to swing dance. Each Thursday at 7:30pm, a company called Dirt Road Dancing comes out to teach beginners how to swing dance. The Buffalo Club, located in Boise, as well as Cowgirls in Kuna, are more of a bar scene for those who are over the age of 21. At the Buffalo Club, Swing Dancing is taught by Country Dance Idaho, or CDID, whereas Dirt Road Dancing teaches at Cowgirls in Kuna as well as Indian Creek Steakhouse.

Basics of Country Swing

When first learning how to swing dance, there are three basic moves that one must learn to look like they know what they're doing on the dance floor. These moves include:

  1. Waistslide
  2. Follow Turn
  3. Underarm Pass
